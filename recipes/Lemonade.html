Lemonade

Description

Lemonade is a refreshing, sweet-tart beverage made primarily from freshly squeezed lemon juice, water, and sugar. Loved for its zesty flavor and cooling effect, it is a popular drink during hot summer days. The balance of sourness from the lemons and sweetness from the sugar makes it both invigorating and hydrating. Often served chilled with ice cubes and garnished with lemon slices or mint leaves, lemonade is not only a thirst-quencher but also a simple, timeless classic enjoyed worldwide.

Ingredients

  • Fresh lemons (for juice and slices)
  • Water (cold or chilled)
  • Sugar (to taste, or substitute with honey)
  • Ice cubes (optional, for serving)
  • Mint leaves (optional, for garnish)

Steps

  1. Juice the lemons– Cut fresh lemons in half and squeeze out the juice into a bowl or jug, removing the seeds.
  2. Mix with sugar– Add sugar to the lemon juice and stir until it dissolves (you can add a little warm water to help it mix faster).
  3. Add water– Pour in chilled water and stir well to combine. Adjust the ratio of lemon, sugar, and water to your taste.
  4. Add ice– Drop in a few ice cubes to keep it cold and refreshing.
  5. Garnish and serve– Add lemon slices or mint leaves on top and serve immediately.
